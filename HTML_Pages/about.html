The Story of Gary Blue's Diner Gary Blue's Diner is a renowned casual dining restaurant that started in the historic district of downtown St. John's, NL. Gary's grandfather, "Big Red", moved from Ireland in the 1850's looking for a better life for him and his family. Big Red's mother was a cook for an Irish landowner, so he spent a lot of time in the kitchen. At the young age of 10, Red was already working full time with his mama; cutting up vegetables, washing dishes but his favorite job was being his Mama's taste tester. His Mama cooked with meats, vegetables and spices he had never tasted before. At the passing of his beloved mother, he took his little family across the ocean looking for a better and brighter life for his family. With little to no education or job experience, Red started working in the back of restaurants washing the dishes. But he had ambition and worked his way up and down Water Street at the various establishments learning the restaurant trade.

Red and his wife, Dolly, scrimped and saved every penny they had in the hopes of one day opening their own little place. Forty years later, at the age of 55, Red and his family had saved enough money to open a little eat-in dinner called "Big R's" after Red himself. Unfortunately, Big Red only lived his dream for 10 years. Red's oldest son, Rick learned to cook from his father but he credit's his mother, Dolly, for teaching him to be hardworking, have dreams and most importantly, no matter how good the food is - customer service made or broke a restaurant. Rick turned Big R's into one of St.John's famous take-out.

Unlike his father and grandfather before him, Gary spent his days in school but any spare time he had; he was at the restaurant. Gary followed in his family footsteps and opened his own restaurant "Gary Blue's Diner". The restaurant is a family-owned and operated full-service restaurant. Gary's philosophy centers around each customer individually. He wants their meal to be something they remember long after the last bite. Focusing on fresh local ingredients and attention to deal have contributed the success and continual growth of the restaurant. As Gary's family grows so does his business with the opening of his new location in CBS which is run by his youngest daughter, Regina named after her great grandmother.

Come celebrate with us and join the family!
